# Email Principles - CirrusLabs Outreach

**Used by:** Skill 3 (Campaign Copy) to generate email variants
**Tone:** Direct, specific, evidence-based, no fluff
**Length:** 100-150 words (2-3 short paragraphs)
**Goal:** Get reply or meeting, not sign a contract

---

## What Works (✅ DO THIS)

### Structure that Works
```
Paragraph 1: Lead with the signal (1 sentence)
Paragraph 2: Why we're different / quick proof (2-3 sentences)
Paragraph 3: CTA - specific time or action (1-2 sentences)
```

### Opening Lines That Work

✅ **Reference the hiring signal:**
- "I saw [Company] is hiring [Role]..."
- "I noticed you posted a job for [Role] last week..."
- "Saw [Company] is looking for [Role] by Q2..."

✅ **Reference funding/growth signal:**
- "Congrats on your Series B! This is typically when companies start scaling engineering..."
- "I see you just announced $XX funding. Usually followed by hiring..."

✅ **Reference leadership signal:**
- "As the new VP of Engineering, you probably need to build out your team..."

✅ **Never start with:**
- ❌ "Hope this finds you well"
- ❌ "I came across your profile"
- ❌ "I think you'd be a great fit"
- ❌ "Let me introduce myself"

### Body Copy That Works

✅ **Specific mention of the problem:**
- "Finding senior Data Engineers takes most companies 3-4 months recruiting."
- "Growing teams need new hires yesterday, not next quarter."
- "Migration projects always need more Cloud architects than you expect."

✅ **Proof of competence:**
- "We specialize in [role] at companies like yours."
- "Last quarter we placed 4 Data Engineers at Series B/C companies."
- "We work specifically with CTOs and VPs who need people fast."

✅ **Show understanding of their world:**
- "With 200 employees, you're hitting the growth pains where recruiting bottlenecks product."
- "Pre-Series B, hiring is usually founder+CEO. Post-funding, you need pros."

✅ **Why us, not alternatives:**
- "Most recruiting takes 3-4 months. We do it in 3-4 weeks."
- "We pre-vet our candidates so interviews matter more."
- "You don't pay unless we place someone."

### CTAs That Work

✅ **Specific, with two options:**
- "Does Tuesday 2pm or Thursday 10am work for a 15-min intro?"
- "Could grab 20 min this week to discuss your timeline?"
- "Quick call this Thursday or Friday?"

✅ **Low friction:**
- "Just want to learn about your plans, no sales pitch."
- "30 min max—I'll dial in to you."
- "Let's see if it makes sense to work together."

✅ **Never say:**
- ❌ "Let me know if interested"
- ❌ "Reach out if you want to chat"
- ❌ "Would love to connect"

---

## What Doesn't Work (❌ DON'T DO THIS)

❌ **Generic filler:**
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "I was impressed by your company"
- "I think we could really help you"
- "I've been following your company"

❌ **Vague claims:**
- "We have amazing engineers"
- "We're a thought leader in staffing"
- "We've changed lives"
- "Revolutionary approach"

❌ **Long rambling:**
- Multiple paragraphs explaining your company history
- Five different reasons why they should talk to you
- Case studies in cold email

❌ **Presumptuous:**
- "I'm reaching out because I noticed you need help" (pushy)
- "I can solve your biggest problem" (you don't know their biggest problem)
- "This is time-sensitive" (creates urgency fake)

❌ **Wrong tone:**
- Overly formal: "I would like to petition your time"
- Overly casual: "Yo, saw you're hiring bro"
- Overly salesy: "Seriously, this is an amazing opportunity"

## Email Template Variants

### VARIANT 1: Job Posting Lead (Strongest Signal)

```
Hi [First Name],

I saw [Company] posted a job for [Role] last week. Finding [role plural]
takes most companies 3-4 months. We typically do it in 3-4 weeks.

We specialize in placing [role plural] at companies like yours.
Last quarter we filled 4 roles at Series B/C companies in your space.

Does Tuesday 2pm or Thursday 10am work for a quick 15-min call?

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Leads with observable signal (job posting)
- Shows understanding of their problem (recruiting timeline)
- Proof of competence (previous placements)
- Specific CTA (two times offered)

---

### VARIANT 2: Funding Lead (Medium Signal)

```
Hi [First Name],

Congrats on your Series B! We work with companies at your stage who
are scaling engineering teams. Most start hiring 2-3 months after funding.

We've built out teams for companies transitioning from founder-led hiring
to dedicated recruiting. Typically fills key roles in 3-4 weeks vs
3-4 months the traditional way.

Could grab 20 min this week to see if it makes sense?

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges milestone (funding)
- Speaks to their specific stage challenges
- Proof (success with similar companies)
- Low-pressure ask

---

### VARIANT 3: Growth Signal Lead (Good Signal)

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] has been growing fast—[specifics]. When teams
scale, hiring usually becomes the bottleneck.

We specialize in scaling engineering teams for companies at your stage.
We focus on [Role] and [Role]—the hardest to find.

Would make sense to chat about your current hiring plans?

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Shows you did research (growth trajectory)
- Frames it as common problem at their stage
- Specific about what we do (certain roles)

---

### VARIANT 4: Pod Selling Angle (Multiple Roles)

```
Hi [First Name],

I see you're hiring for Data Engineer, Data Scientist, and MLOps Engineer.
Most companies hire these one-by-one over 6+ months.

We specialize in building complete AI/ML teams. Full squad placed,
on-boarded, and productive in 4-6 weeks. Volume discount applies.

Interested in exploring what a complete team placement could look like?

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Spots multiple roles (pod opportunity)
- Frames as team problem, not individual roles
- Offers different value (fast team vs slow individuals)
- Volume angle (discount)

---

### VARIANT 5: Problem-First Angle (No Signal Yet, Generic ICP)

```
Hi [First Name],

[Company] is at an inflection point—you need to scale engineering
to hit growth targets. The challenge: recruiting takes 3-4 months
and you don't have that time.

This is where we come in. We fill roles in 3-4 weeks for companies like you.

Let's talk about how we can help accelerate your timeline.

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Speaks to their situation even without signal
- Addresses the core pain (time pressure)
- Establishes your differentiation
- Natural progression to CTA

## Subject Lines That Work

✅ **Good subject lines:**
- "[Company] + [Role] = ?"
- "Data Engineer hiring at [Company]?"
- "Quick question on your [Role] search"
- "[Company] - 3 week hiring timeline?"
- "Saw [Company] is expanding engineering"

✅ **What subject lines should do:**
- Make them curious
- Reference their company (not generic)
- Hint at our value ("3 week timeline")
- Invite response (question mark)

❌ **Bad subject lines:**
- "Staffing for Your Company"
- "Let's Connect"
- "Opportunity"
- "Interested?"
- "Sales Pitch Inside"

**Testing:** Try 2-3 subject line variants. See which gets higher open rate.

## Key Principles

### 1. Lead With Signal (Most Important)
The strongest emails reference something observable we found about them:
- Job posting ✅ (strongest)
- Funding announcement ✅ (strong)
- Leadership change ✅ (strong)
- Growth trajectory ✅ (good)
- Company size/stage ✅ (baseline)

### 2. Respect Their Time
- Short (2-3 paragraphs max)
- Clear (not cryptic)
- Actionable (specific CTA)
- Honest (not overselling)

### 3. Speak Their Language
If targeting CTO: "Technical depth, fast hiring"
If targeting VP: "Execution, team productivity"
If targeting Founder: "Speed, results, no BS"

### 4. One Primary Message
Don't try to say 5 things. One main reason to reply:
- "We're faster" OR
- "We're specialists in your roles" OR
- "We take the hiring risk" OR
- "We understand your stage"
Pick one. Lead with it.

### 5. Proof Beats Talk
"We placed 3 data engineers last quarter" beats "We're great"
"3-4 weeks vs 3-4 months" beats "Fast recruiting"
Job URLs beat "I saw you're hiring somewhere"

## By Vertical (Tone Adjustments)

### Data/AI Roles
Focus on: Specialization, team depth, modern stack
Lead: "I saw [Company] is building AI/ML team..."

### Cloud/Infrastructure Roles
Focus on: Migration projects, team capability, timeline
Lead: "Noticed [Company] is likely migrating to cloud..."

### General Software Dev
Focus on: Scaling, productivity, product timeline
Lead: "With [Company]'s growth, engineering scaling matters..."

---

## The Anti-Pattern: What CTOs Hate

❌ **Multiple messages in one week** (spam look)
❌ **Copy-paste generic messages** ("I came across your profile")
❌ **No mention of their company** ("I work with companies like yours")
❌ **Overselling** ("This will transform your team")
❌ **Urgency fake** ("This opportunity won't last")
❌ **Long rambling** (Wall of text)
❌ **Weak CTA** ("Let me know if interested")

## Emergency Fallback

If you don't have a strong signal:

```
Hi [First Name],

[Company] is in a growth phase where engineering speed matters.
We help companies like yours scale technical teams without the
3-4 month recruiting timeline.

Worth a 15-min chat?

[Your name]
```

This works. It's not as strong as signal-based, but it's honest and direct.

## Remember

Your emails represent the CEO's brand and LinkedIn account.
- Be professional but personal
- Be honest but compelling
- Be direct but respectful
- Be specific but brief

The goal is a conversation, not a commitment.
A reply is a win.
A meeting is success.
