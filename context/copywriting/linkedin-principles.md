# LinkedIn Principles - CirrusLabs

**Used by:** Skill 5 (if using LinkedIn as secondary channel)
**CEO Account:** Primary reach channel
**Safety First:** Avoid LinkedIn restrictions and account suspension

---

## LinkedIn Safety Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### Hard Limits
- ❌ **No automation** - LinkedIn bans accounts that use bots
- ❌ **No mass connection requests** - Don't send 100 requests in 1 day
- ❌ **No identical copy-paste** - Vary message slightly each time
- ✅ **Manual sending only** - CEO or team member sends manually
- ✅ **Stagger sends** - Min 5-10 seconds between actions

### Connection Sending Rules
- ✅ Max 50 connection requests per day (safe)
- ✅ Add personalized message when connecting (required by LinkedIn)
- ✅ Wait 2-3 days after connect before DM (gives time to accept)
- ❌ Don't connect + DM same day (looks automated)

### DM Rules
- ✅ Send DMs only to existing connections (don't cold DM strangers)
- ✅ Max 10-15 DMs per day (safe)
- ✅ Vary your messaging (slight differences each time)
- ✅ Don't send DMs at 3am (looks bot-like)
- ❌ Don't retry same person multiple times (persistent spam)

### Content Rules
- ✅ Use own words (not AI-generated sounding)
- ✅ Reference their profile/posts when possible
- ✅ Genuine connection reasons
- ❌ "I saw your profile" (generic)
- ❌ "I think you'd be interested" (salesy)
- ❌ "Let me introduce you to..." (irrelevant)

## LinkedIn DM Strategy (CEO Account)

### Why LinkedIn DMs as Secondary Channel?

**Pros:**
- Higher trust than cold email (shared network)
- Person already sees your profile = credibility
- Follow-up after email (warm touch)
- Good for CTOs who ignore email

**Cons:**
- Requires connection first (2-step process)
- LinkedIn limits message volume
- Looks more personal = higher expectations
- Can't automate (must be CEO)

**Recommendation:** Email first (fast), LinkedIn second (warm-up if no reply in 1 week).

## Connection Request Strategy

### What to Include in Connection Request

✅ **Good connection request:**
```
Message: "Hi John - Saw you're leading engineering at TechCorp.
Specific reason to connect because we both work in tech staffing.
Would be good to connect."
```

✅ **Even better:**
```
Message: "Hi John - I work with companies hiring Data Engineers.
Noticed TechCorp posted a role recently. Would love to connect."
```

❌ **Bad connection request:**
```
No message at all (generic connection request)
```

❌ **Also bad:**
```
"Let me introduce you to..." (weird sales move)
"Check out my profile" (self-focused)
```

### Connection Acceptance Expectations
- 25-50% of connection requests will be accepted
- CTOs: ~40% acceptance (more open to networks)
- Founders: ~35% acceptance (selective)
- Directors: ~45% acceptance (most open)

### Timeline After Connection
- Day 1-2: Wait (let them approve)
- Day 2-3: Send first DM (warm introduction)
- Day 4-7: If no reply, send email instead
- Day 7+: Forget about LinkedIn, focus on email

## LinkedIn DM Templates

### TEMPLATE 1: Warm Intro (After They Accept Connection)

```
Hi John,

Thanks for connecting. I noticed TechCorp is hiring for your engineering
team—specifically [Role]. We specialize in filling roles like this fast
(typically 3-4 weeks vs 3-4 months recruiting).

Might make sense to chat about how we could help accelerate your timeline.

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Thanks for connection (personal touch)
- References hiring (specific)
- Shows speed advantage
- Soft CTA

---

### TEMPLATE 2: Value-First (If Email Didn't Get Reply)

```
Hi John,

I realize you might not have seen my email. Wanted to reach out directly
since I think this might actually be relevant.

[Company] is hiring [role]—typically takes 3-4 months with traditional
recruiting. We do it in 3-4 weeks because we start with vetted candidates,
not job posts.

No pressure to chat, but thought worth flagging.

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges possible silence (not pushy)
- Frames as helpful (not salesy)
- Explains difference
- Honest tone

---

### TEMPLATE 3: Shared Interest (If You Know They Posted)

```
Hi John,

Saw your recent post about [topic]. Totally agree with [specific point].

Also noticed TechCorp is scaling the engineering team. That's always the
hard part. We typically help companies accelerate that timeline.

Anyway, good to connect here. Let's stay in touch.

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Shows you actually read their post
- Compliments their thinking
- Casually mentions hiring context
- Leaves door open

## What LinkedIn is (NOT) For

### ✅ LinkedIn IS Good For:
- Secondary outreach (after email fails)
- CEO/founder relationship building
- Warm introductions (through network)
- Profile credibility (for email recipients to check)
- Following up if email bounced
- Social proof (having real followers)

### ❌ LinkedIn is NOT For:
- Primary cold outreach (too limited)
- Scaling (max 15-20 DMs per day = slow)
- Automated sequences (LinkedIn bans bots)
- Spamming connections
- Sending the same message to everyone

## LinkedIn Profile Setup (CEO Account)

### Profile Elements That Help Replies

✅ **Professional photo** - Headshot, professional dress, good lighting
✅ **Headline** - "[CEO/Founder of CirrusLabs] | Tech Staffing"
✅ **About section** - Clear value prop:
```
"We place specialized engineers (Data, AI, Cloud) at growth-stage tech companies.
If you're hiring for engineering roles, let's talk."
```

✅ **Recent activity** - Post 1-2x per week (shows active, not dormant account)
✅ **Endorsements** - Ask team to endorse staffing, recruiting, tech
✅ **Recommendations** - Get 2-3 from past clients

### What Helps People Respond
- Real photo (not generic avatar)
- Active profile (recent updates/posts)
- Clear what you do (not vague)
- Consistent tone (professional but personable)

## LinkedIn Message Length

### Character Limits
- Connection request message: 300 characters max (be brief)
- DM: No hard limit, but 1-2 short paragraphs works best
- Not an email - keep it conversational

### Rule of Thumb
```
SHORT (3-4 lines): Best reply rate
MEDIUM (5-7 lines): Good reply rate
LONG (10+ lines): Low reply rate (too much to read on mobile)
```

## Safety Checklist Before Sending

Before CEO sends a connection request or DM, check:

- [ ] **Not spamming same person** - Haven't contacted them in past 30 days
- [ ] **Relevant connection** - They're actually in tech hiring
- [ ] **Personalized** - Slight variation from last DM
- [ ] **Not 3am send** (schedule for business hours 9am-5pm)
- [ ] **Genuine tone** - Reads like real human, not template
- [ ] **No urgency fake** - Not "this won't last" or "act now"
- [ ] **Easy to say no** - Not pushy (gives them out)

## LinkedIn vs Email Comparison

| Factor | Email | LinkedIn |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Reach | Higher (reach everyone) | Limited (connections only) |
| Reply Rate | 20-30% (signal-based) | 25-40% (warmer channel) |
| Speed | Fast (instant) | Slower (2-3 day delay) |
| Scale | Can send 100+ per day | Max 15-20 per day |
| Automation | Easily automated | Can't automate (ban risk) |
| Risk | Low (if verified emails) | High (account suspension) |
| Best For | Initial outreach | Follow-up / warm touchx |

**Strategy:** Email = primary, LinkedIn = secondary/follow-up only

## If LinkedIn Account Gets Restricted

### Warning Signs
- Messages sent slowly (LinkedIn throttling you)
- Profile views drop significantly
- Can't send connection requests
- "Unusual activity" warning from LinkedIn

### What To Do
- Stop all outreach for 2-3 weeks
- Post normal activity (articles, comments)
- Don't send any connection requests or DMs
- Wait for LinkedIn to clear the flag
- Resume slowly (5 connections/day max)

### Prevention
- Never mass-add connections (no more than 50/day)
- Never send identical messages
- Always wait 2-3 days after connecting before DM
- Use proper business hours only
- Keep profile active (posts, comments, regular activity)

## LinkedIn for Team (If Scaling Beyond CEO)

If you hire a "LinkedIn specialist" to help:

✅ **Safe approach:**
- Multiple accounts (not CEO's main account)
- Set up new profiles for team members
- Manual sending only (no scheduling tools)
- Different message variants (not copy-paste)
- Stagger sends (5-10 second gaps)
- Business hours only (9am-5pm)

❌ **Dangerous approach:**
- Automation tools
- Identical messages
- Mass uploads
- Sending at odd hours (looks bot-like)
- Same person sending 50+ per day

## LinkedIn + Email Sequence (Optional)

If doing both channels:

```
Day 1: Email with signal (job posting link)
  - Subject: "[Company] + [Role]?"
  - Body: 3 paragraphs

Day 3-5: LinkedIn connection request (if hasn't replied)
  - Message: Brief note about hiring

Day 7: LinkedIn DM (if they accepted)
  - Warm follow-up about timing

Day 10: Email follow-up
  - "Following up on my email last week..."

Day 14: Stop (move on to next person)
```

This is a warm sequence. Safe. Not automated.

## Remember

LinkedIn is a **credibility channel**, not a **scale channel**.

- ✅ Use it to show you're real (profile, connections)
- ✅ Use it as backup if email doesn't work
- ✅ Use it for warm introductions through network
- ❌ Don't use it for mass outreach
- ❌ Don't automate it
- ❌ Don't copy-paste identical messages

The CEO's LinkedIn account is an asset worth protecting.

One account ban = months of relationship-building lost.

Not worth the risk.

**Recommendation:** Focus on email (safe, scalable). LinkedIn is nice-to-have follow-up.
