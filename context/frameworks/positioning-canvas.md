## Template: Positioning Canvas for TaaS Offer

### 1. Category
**Question:** What type of service is this?

**Our Answer:**
```
Talent As A Service (TaaS)
- Specific definition: Flexible, pre-vetted engineering staffing for tech roles
- Not: Recruiting agency (we don't just match resumes)
- Not: Consulting (we're not advising, we're providing people)
- Is: On-demand, vetted talent pool available on short notice
```

### 2. Target Customer
**Question:** Who are we selling to? (Be specific)

**Our Answer:**
```
Primary: CTOs and VPs of Engineering at:
  - Startups with $20M+ funding (Series A and above)
  - SMBs with 50-1000 employees and $5M+ ARR
  - Enterprises with 1000+ employees

Secondary: Directors of Engineering, CIOs

Geographic: US (primary), LATAM (Brazil, Mexico, Colombia)

Verticals: Any company hiring for Data, AI, Cloud, or Software Dev roles
```

### 3. Customer Problem (Observable Trigger)
**Question:** What specific pain point makes them say "yes"?

**Our Answer:**
```
PRIMARY TRIGGER: Active hiring
"We posted a job for Data Engineer 2 weeks ago and can't find qualified candidates"

Related triggers:
- "We need someone by next month but recruiting takes 4-6 months"
- "We had an engineer quit and need a replacement"
- "We're scaling the team but our recruiting is bottlenecked"
- "We need specialized skills (ML, Cloud, Data) that are hard to find"

What we DON'T solve:
- Long-term hiring (we're on-demand, not permanent hiring process)
- Non-technical hiring (sales, marketing, operations)
- Roles outside Data/AI/Cloud/Software
```

### 4. Why Now (Timeliness)
**Question:** Why should they care TODAY?

**Our Answer:**
```
Timing factors:
- Job posting is 0-7 days old (they're actively recruiting)
- Quarter is starting (budget approved)
- They just raised funding (hiring wave incoming)
- Headcount dropped (backfill urgency)
- New CTO/VP (mandate to build team)

Our advantage RIGHT NOW:
- Recruiting cycle: 8-12 weeks
- Our turnaround: 1-2 weeks
- They NEED people before Q2 OKRs
```

### 5. Customer Alternative (What They'd Do Instead)
**Question:** What's their current solution? What are they comparing us to?

**Our Answer:**
```
Alternatives they consider:
1. Traditional recruiting (3-6 months, expensive)
2. DIY LinkedIn recruiting (CEO/CTO's time = expensive)
3. Recruiting agencies (high fee, not always vetted)
4. Internal promotions (no new headcount)
5. Freelance/contract (low commitment, temporary)

Why our solution beats each:
1. vs Recruiting → 10x faster, results in weeks
2. vs DIY recruiting → CEO's time freed up for strategy
3. vs Recruiting agencies → We specialize in tech, higher quality
4. vs Promotion → Doesn't help new capacity
5. vs Freelance → Full-time, committed team members
```

### 6. Observable Success Signal (How They'll Know It Works)
**Question:** What would success look like? How would they measure it?

**Our Answer:**
```
For us to have succeeded:
- Role filled within 3-4 weeks (vs 8-12 weeks normal)
- Person is on-boarded and productive within 2 weeks
- Retention at 18+ months (we hired well)
- No bad hires that had to be let go

Measurable outcomes:
- "We shipped feature X on time because we had the engineer"
- "Our data platform improved because we got a senior DE"
- "The new person trained the team on cloud architecture"

This is what we commit to in the SLA
```

### 7. Primary Value Proposition (One Line)
**Question:** If we had 10 seconds, what's the core reason to buy?

**Our Answer:**
```
SHORT VERSION:
"We fill engineering roles in 3-4 weeks when recruiting would take 3-4 months."

MEDIUM VERSION:
"We're a pre-vetted talent pool for Data, AI, Cloud, and Software Dev roles.
We specialize in getting the right people on your team fast."

LONG VERSION:
"Most companies spend 8-12 weeks recruiting engineers.
We do it in 3-4 weeks by starting with vetted candidates, not job posts.
We take the hiring risk—you only pay if we place them.
We specialize in tech roles (Data, AI, Cloud, Software Dev) where it's hardest to find people.
We work with CTOs and VPs who need headcount now, not 6 months from now."
```

### 8. Key Differentiators (Why Us, Not Them?)
**Question:** What makes us different from other staffing/recruiting companies?

**Our Answer:**
```
1. Speed
   - Us: 3-4 weeks to hire
   - Industry: 8-12 weeks
   - How: We pre-vet, we start with proof (hiring signals), we're focused

2. Specialization
   - Us: Only tech roles (Data, AI, Cloud, Software)
   - Competitors: "We hire everyone"
   - Why it matters: Tech hiring is different; we understand the roles

3. Quality
   - Us: Only place candidates we'd hire ourselves
   - Industry: Resume matching
   - Proof: Our candidates stay 18+ months (retention SLA)

4. Flexibility
   - Us: Pod selling (hire a team) or individual placements
   - Industry: One-at-a-time recruiting
   - Why: Some companies need 1 person, some need 5

5. Risk Transfer
   - Us: We take hiring risk
   - Industry: Company pays upfront, hopes it works out
   - Our model: You pay when hired, stay if they succeed, replacement if they don't
```

### 9. Sales Model (How We Work)
**Question:** What's the engagement model?

**Our Answer:**
```
INDIVIDUAL PLACEMENT:
- You need: 1 Data Engineer
- We deliver: 1 vetted engineer within 3-4 weeks
- You pay: Fee when hired, bonus if they stay 12+ months
- Guaranteed: Replacement if they leave within 6 months

POD SELLING (TEAM):
- You need: Full AI/ML team (3-5 people)
- We deliver: Vetted team, integrated, on-boarded together
- You pay: Per-person fee, volume discount
- Guaranteed: Team stability for 12+ months

TIMELINE:
Week 1: Brief us on requirements
Week 1-2: We source candidates
Week 2-3: Interviews & selection
Week 3-4: Onboarding starts
```

```
OBJECTION 4: "Are they full-time or contract?"

but they work for you, report to you, get your benefits."

OBJECTION 5: "How much does it cost?"
RESPONSE: "Depends on role and level. Data Engineer ranges from $X-Y.
Volume discounts for pod selling. Discuss specifics after intro call."
```

## Key Principles for Positioning

1. **Specificity matters** - "Staffing for tech" is vague. "We fill Data Eng roles in 3-4 weeks" is specific.
2. **Tie to observable signal** - Base positioning on what we can actually detect (hiring signals)
3. **One primary pain point** - Focus on the #1 reason they'd say yes (speed of hiring)
4. **Clear alternative** - Define what they're comparing us to (recruiting, DIY hiring)
5. **Measurable success** - How will they know we succeeded? (Candidate hired + productive in 30 days)
