# Mistakes to Avoid

## Copy Mistakes

### 1. Starting with "I"
❌ "I wanted to reach out about..."
✅ "Noticed [Company] is hiring [Role]..."

Starting with yourself signals that you're asking before giving. Start with them.

### 2. Vague value propositions
❌ "We help companies hire great engineers."
✅ "We place senior Data Engineers in 3-4 weeks, not 3-4 months."

Specificity converts. Vague claims don't.

### 3. Long emails
❌ 250+ word emails with multi-paragraph pitches
✅ 75-150 words max for cold outreach

Attention span for cold email is 5 seconds. Use all of them.

### 4. Asking for a 30-minute call
❌ "Would you have 30 minutes for a discovery call?"
✅ "Worth a 10-minute intro this week or next?"

Lower the friction. Smaller ask = higher conversion.

### 5. Using buzzwords
❌ "We leverage cutting-edge talent solutions to optimize your hiring pipeline."
✅ "We find and place the engineers you're trying to hire."

Speak like a human, not a LinkedIn post.

### 6. Generic subject lines
❌ "Following up", "Introduction", "Quick question"
✅ "[Company]'s [Role] search — 3-4 week option?"

The subject line is the only thing that gets you opened. Make it specific.

## Targeting Mistakes

### 7. Targeting companies that are too small
❌ Companies with < 10 employees — no budget, no process
✅ 50-500 employees for best conversion

### 8. Not filtering by signal freshness
❌ Job posts 90+ days old
✅ Job posts < 30 days old (pain is fresh)

### 9. Targeting competitors
❌ Staffing firms, recruiting agencies
✅ Tech companies that need to hire engineers

### 10. Wrong decision-maker
❌ HR Manager, Recruiter (gatekeeper)
✅ CTO, VP Engineering, Founder (budget holder, decision-maker)

---

## Apollo Sequence Mistakes

### 11. Too many steps
❌ 10-step sequence over 30 days
✅ 3-step sequence: Day 0, Day 3, Day 7, then stop

Persistence is good; harassment is not. 3 touches is the sweet spot.

### 12. Identical follow-ups
❌ "Just following up on my last email..."
✅ Each email adds new angle, new proof point, or asks a different question

### 13. Sending from generic mailboxes
❌ info@cirrusconsulting.com
✅ [FirstName]@cirrusconsulting.com

Personal sender name = higher open rates and credibility.

---

## Process Mistakes

### 14. Not verifying emails
❌ Sending to unverified emails
✅ Only send to `verified` or `likely to engage` status in Apollo

Bounces hurt deliverability. One high-bounce campaign can tank your domain.

### 15. Scaling before validating
❌ Sending 500 emails with untested copy
✅ Test 20-30 first, measure reply rate, then scale

Always validate copy before scale.

### 16. Not learning from replies
❌ Only tracking "positive" or "negative"
✅ Tag every reply: positive, negative, objection, referral, OOO

Objections are gold — they tell you what to address in future copy.

### 17. Skipping Skill 6
❌ Launching campaign → forgetting to analyze results
✅ Run Skill 6 after every campaign, update what-works.md

The flywheel only works if you feed the learnings back in.

---

## The Most Important Rule

**Never send an email you wouldn't be proud to receive.**

If you'd be annoyed getting your own email, rewrite it.
