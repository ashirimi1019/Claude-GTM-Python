# Use-Case Driven Outreach Principle

## Core Idea

**Match your message to a specific use case, not a general pitch.**

Generic: "We place software engineers."
Use-case driven: "We place senior Data Engineers for Series B/C startups that are building their first data platform."

The second one is 10x more likely to get a reply from the right person.

---

## Why Use Cases Beat General Pitches

When a CTO reads your email, they're pattern-matching:
*"Is this relevant to my specific situation right now?"*

If you're generic, the pattern fails to match. Deleted.
If you're specific to their use case, it clicks. They read on.

## CirrusLabs Use Cases (By Signal)

### Use Case 1: Data Engineering Team Build
**Signal:** Company hiring 2+ Data Engineers
**Buyer:** Head of Data, VP Engineering
**Pain:** Data pipeline backlog, 90-day hiring cycle, no senior talent pipeline
**Message angle:** "We've placed DE teams at 3 Series B startups in the last quarter — same profile you're hiring for."

### Use Case 2: ML Productionization
**Signal:** Company hiring ML Engineers or AI Engineers
**Buyer:** CTO, Head of AI
**Pain:** Models stuck in notebooks, no MLOps, need production engineers fast
**Message angle:** "ML Engineers who can ship to prod are 3x harder to find than regular SWEs — we specialize in exactly this profile."

### Use Case 3: Cloud/Platform Engineering
**Signal:** Company hiring Cloud Engineers, DevOps, SRE, Platform
**Buyer:** CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Platform
**Pain:** AWS costs out of control, no internal cloud expertise, too slow to ship infra
**Message angle:** "We've placed senior Platform Engineers at high-growth SaaS companies that needed to scale infra without slowing down product."

### Use Case 4: Backend/Full Stack Scale
**Signal:** Company hiring Backend Engineers, Full Stack Engineers
**Buyer:** CTO, VP Engineering
**Pain:** Product roadmap blocked by engineering capacity
**Message angle:** "We've helped engineering teams double output in 60 days with senior contract engineers while you run your permanent search in parallel."

---

## How to Select the Right Use Case

1. Look at the hiring signal (job titles they're posting)
2. Match to the closest use case above
3. Identify the primary pain that use case solves
4. Lead with that pain in the email

---

## Use Case Template

For any new campaign, define:

```
Use Case Name: _______________
Signal that triggers it: _______________
Buyer title(s): _______________
Primary pain: _______________
What we deliver: _______________
Proof point (if available): _______________
Key message angle: _______________
```

---

## Application in Copy Generation (Skill 3)

When generating email variants, the OpenAI prompt should receive:
1. The **use case** as context
2. The **specific signal** as the hook
3. The **pain** to reference
4. Any **proof points** from `what-works.md`

This ensures generated copy is use-case specific, not generic.
