# AI & Data Consulting — Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary Tier: Mid-Market to Enterprise Building AI/Data Capabilities

Companies that have product-market fit and revenue traction but are early-to-mid in their data maturity journey. They know data and AI matter but lack the internal bench strength to execute well.

## Firmographics

### Company Size
- **Sweet spot:** 200-5,000 employees
- **Revenue:** $20M-$500M ARR (or equivalent for non-SaaS)
- **Why this range:** Large enough to have real data problems and budget for external help, small enough that they haven't built a 50-person data org yet

### Funding / Financial Profile
- Series B through Series D (venture-backed)
- Growth equity or PE-backed companies scaling operations
- Public mid-caps investing in digital transformation
- Profitable private companies with $5M+ annual technology budget

### Industry Verticals (Strongest Fit)
- **Fintech / Financial Services** — Regulatory data requirements, fraud/risk ML, real-time analytics
- **Healthcare / Life Sciences** — Clinical data platforms, patient analytics, regulatory compliance
- **E-commerce / Marketplace** — Recommendation engines, demand forecasting, customer analytics
- **SaaS / B2B Tech** — Product analytics, usage-based pricing data, churn prediction
- **Insurance** — Claims analytics, underwriting models, actuarial data modernization
- **Logistics / Supply Chain** — Route optimization, demand forecasting, IoT data pipelines

### Geography
- Primary: United States (all major tech hubs + distributed companies)
- Secondary: UK, Singapore, Australia (English-speaking markets with similar data maturity patterns)

## Technographics — Data Stack Signals

### Positive Signals (Modern Stack Adoption)
These indicate the company is investing in data but may need help getting value:
- **Cloud data warehouse:** Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, Redshift
- **Data transformation:** dbt, Dataform
- **Orchestration:** Airflow, Dagster, Prefect
- **BI/Analytics:** Looker, Tableau, Mode, Metabase
- **ML platforms:** MLflow, SageMaker, Vertex AI, Weights & Biases
- **Data catalog:** Atlan, Alation, DataHub

### Strongest Signal Combinations
- Snowflake + no dbt = data warehouse adopted but pipeline maturity is low
- Databricks + job postings for ML engineers = trying to productionize ML
- BigQuery + Looker + hiring VP Data = scaling analytics, need leadership
- Airflow + "data quality" in job descriptions = pipeline reliability problems

### Negative Technographic Signals
- Fully on-premise data stack with no cloud migration plans
- Using only Excel/Google Sheets for analytics (too early-stage)
- Already running Palantir, Databricks Unity Catalog, and a 30+ person data team (too mature)

## Behavioral Signals

### High-Intent Indicators
1. **Hiring for first senior data role** — CDO, VP Data, Head of Analytics, Head of ML
2. **Posting multiple data engineering roles simultaneously** — Building a team from scratch
3. **Job descriptions mention "greenfield" or "from scratch"** — Need architecture, not just bodies
4. **Leadership speaking about AI at conferences or in press** — Executive sponsorship exists
5. **Recent funding round with AI/data mentioned in announcement** — Budget allocated
6. **Posted and then removed data leadership role** — Search failed, may need fractional help
7. **Glassdoor/Blind complaints about data quality or tooling** — Pain is real and felt internally

### Medium-Intent Indicators
- Blog posts about data stack decisions
- Engineers posting about data challenges on social media
- Company appearing on "modern data stack" community lists
- Attending data conferences (Snowflake Summit, dbt Coalesce, Data Council)

## Organizational Patterns That Indicate Fit

### Best Fit
- Data team is 2-8 people embedded within engineering, no dedicated data leadership
- Company has 1 senior data person who is overwhelmed and needs strategic support
- Engineering-led company where data was an afterthought and now needs to catch up
- Recently appointed CDO/VP Data who needs to build their function and wants external help

### Good Fit
- Analytics team exists but is reactive (dashboard factory), wants to become strategic
- Company tried building ML internally, struggled, and is now looking for external expertise
- Data team exists but is all junior — needs senior architecture and mentorship

### Poor Fit (Disqualifiers)
- **Mature data org (20+ data professionals)** — They build, don't buy consulting
- **No data budget** — If there's no line item for data investment, there's no deal
- **"Just need a contractor"** — Looking for staff aug, not consulting (route to staffing vertical)
- **No executive sponsor** — Data initiatives without C-level backing stall and die
- **Pre-revenue startup** — Can't afford consulting, shouldn't be spending on it
- **Heavily regulated with no cloud approval** — Government/defense with air-gapped environments
- **Already engaged with Big 4 on same scope** — Competing against incumbent consulting relationship

## Decision-Making Patterns

- **Budget holder:** VP Data, CDO, CTO, or VP Engineering (depends on org structure)
- **Influencer:** Senior data engineers, ML leads, analytics managers
- **Blocker:** Procurement (especially enterprise), legal (data access concerns), CISO (security review)
- **Typical sales cycle:** 4-8 weeks for mid-market, 8-16 weeks for enterprise
- **Common entry point:** Assessment/discovery engagement ($15-30K) that proves value before larger commitment

## Summary: What Makes a Great AI & Data Consulting Lead

The ideal company is:
- Growing (200-5,000 employees, well-funded)
- Data-aware but under-resourced (small data team relative to company size)
- Actively investing (hiring data roles, adopting modern data tools)
- Executive-sponsored (CDO/VP Data exists or is being hired)
- Implementation-ready (not just exploring — they have budget and urgency)
