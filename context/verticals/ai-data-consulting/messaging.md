# AI & Data Consulting — Messaging Framework

## Core Positioning Statement

CirrusLabs delivers practitioner-led AI and data consulting for companies that need more than bodies — they need strategy, architecture, and implementation delivered by people who've done it before. We bridge the gap between the Big 4 (expensive, strategy-heavy) and staffing firms (no ownership, no architecture). We build with you, transfer knowledge, and leave you self-sufficient.

## Key Differentiators to Weave Into All Messaging

1. **"We don't just send bodies, we architect solutions"** — Separates us from staffing
2. **"We build, not just advise"** — Separates us from Big 4 / McKinsey-style consultants
3. **"Tangible deliverable in 30 days"** — Reduces perceived risk
4. **"Your team learns while we build"** — Knowledge transfer as a feature, not an afterthought
5. **"Practitioners, not career consultants"** — Our people have built production data platforms

## Value Propositions by Persona

### For VP Data / CDO
**Primary value prop:** Accelerate your data platform build from months to weeks with a team that's done it before.
**Supporting points:**
- We've designed and implemented data platforms at [scale/industry] — we bring pattern recognition your team doesn't have yet
- Your first 90 days as data leader are critical. We help you show impact fast while building for the long term
- We complement your hiring, not compete with it — our goal is to make your permanent team productive faster

### For CTO (Acting as Data Leader)
**Primary value prop:** Get your data infrastructure to a state where it stops blocking product development.
**Supporting points:**
- Your engineering team shouldn't be maintaining brittle data pipelines — let us fix the foundation
- We'll help you define what a data function looks like for your company and help you hire the right leader
- Fixed scope, defined deliverables, no open-ended consulting — we respect engineering culture

### For VP Analytics / Head of BI
**Primary value prop:** Move your analytics team from reactive dashboard requests to proactive business intelligence.
**Supporting points:**
- We'll fix the data quality issues that are eroding trust in your numbers
- Self-serve analytics isn't a tool purchase — it's an operating model change. We'll design and implement both
- Your analysts become 3x more productive when they're not cleaning data manually

### For Head of ML
**Primary value prop:** Get your models into production with proper MLOps infrastructure.
**Supporting points:**
- Models in notebooks aren't delivering business value. We build the platform that takes them to production
- Feature stores, model registries, monitoring, retraining pipelines — the infrastructure ML teams need but rarely have time to build
- We pair-program with your ML engineers, so they own the platform when we leave

## Subject Line Patterns

### Signal-Referenced (Highest Open Rate)
- "Your [Snowflake/Databricks] investment + [Company]'s data team"
- "Saw [Company] is hiring a [VP Data / Head of ML] — we help with the interim"
- "[Company]'s data engineering buildout"
- "Re: [Company]'s [Series C / funding round] and data platform plans"

### Pain-Referenced
- "When the data team is a bottleneck"
- "[Company]'s data pipeline reliability"
- "Getting ML models past the notebook stage at [Company]"
- "The gap between buying Snowflake and getting value from it"

### Outcome-Referenced
- "Data platform in 12 weeks at [Company]"
- "From ad-hoc requests to self-serve analytics"
- "3 production ML models in 6 months"

### Patterns to Avoid
- "Unlock the power of AI" (generic, empty)
- "Digital transformation" (meaningless buzzword)
- "Synergize your data strategy" (consultant-speak)
- "Quick call?" as the entire subject line (too salesy)
- "AI is the future" (everyone knows this, it's not useful)

## Opening Hook Patterns

### The Observation Hook
> "I noticed [Company] recently posted for [3 data engineering roles / a VP of Data / an ML platform engineer]. Companies at your stage often hit a pattern we see frequently — the team is growing but the data foundation wasn't designed for this scale."

### The Specific Pain Hook
> "When companies adopt [Snowflake/Databricks] but haven't invested in data modeling and pipeline orchestration, the warehouse becomes an expensive lake of ungoverned data. I'm curious whether [Company] is running into this."

### The Peer Pattern Hook
> "We've worked with several [Series C / growth-stage / fintech] companies navigating the transition from 'we have a data warehouse' to 'we have a data platform.' The most common gap we see is [missing data quality layer / no MLOps / analytics team stuck in dashboard mode]."

### The Trigger Event Hook
> "Congratulations on [Company]'s recent [Series C / funding round / CDO hire]. The first 90 days after [funding / a new data leader joining] are typically when the data platform architecture decisions get made. We help companies like [Company] make those decisions well."

### Hooks to Avoid
- "I'd love to pick your brain" (what's in it for them?)
- "We're an AI consulting firm that..." (leading with yourself)
- "Are you leveraging AI yet?" (condescending)
- "I have a solution for your data problems" (you don't know their problems yet)

## Body Copy Structure

### Recommended Flow (3-4 paragraphs max)
1. **Signal reference** — What you observed about their company (1-2 sentences)
2. **Pattern recognition** — What companies like theirs typically experience (2-3 sentences)
3. **Credibility anchor** — What you've done that's relevant, briefly (1-2 sentences)
4. **Low-friction CTA** — Ask for conversation, not commitment (1 sentence)

### Proof Insertion Points
After the pattern recognition paragraph, insert ONE proof point:
- "When we helped a [similar-size / similar-industry] company with this, they [specific outcome]."
- "Typical engagement for this scenario: [scope, timeline, outcome]."
- Use bracket templates from proof-points.md — fill in with real case studies as they become available.

### Tone Guidelines
- **Consultative, not salesy** — You're a peer discussing a shared problem, not pitching
- **Specific, not generic** — Reference their actual stack, roles, signals
- **Confident but not arrogant** — "We see this pattern often" not "We're the best in the world"
- **Respectful of their expertise** — They know their business; you bring external pattern recognition
- **Concise** — 100-150 words for email body. Every sentence must earn its place

## CTA Patterns

### Low Friction (Best for Cold Outreach)
- "Would a 20-minute conversation about [specific topic] be useful?"
- "Happy to share what we've seen work for companies at your stage — interested?"
- "If this resonates, I'm around [Tuesday or Thursday] for a quick call."

### Medium Friction (For Warm or Responded Leads)
- "We could put together a lightweight assessment of your current data architecture — no cost, takes about a week. Interested?"
- "I can share a framework we use for data platform evaluation — want me to send it over?"

### CTAs to Avoid
- "Let's hop on a call!" (too eager)
- "When are you free?" (puts burden on them)
- "Can I send you a proposal?" (too early)
- "Let me show you a demo" (you don't have a demo for consulting)

## LinkedIn Messaging (Secondary Channel)

### Connection Request Note (300 chars max)
> "Hi [First Name] — saw [Company] is building out the data team. We work with [similar companies] on data platform strategy and implementation. Would be great to connect."

### Follow-Up DM (After Connection Accepted)
> "Thanks for connecting, [First Name]. I noticed [specific signal — hiring patterns, tech stack, funding]. We help companies at [Company]'s stage get their data platform to a place where the team can focus on analytics and ML instead of pipeline maintenance. Worth a conversation?"

### LinkedIn Rules
- Max 5-10 outreach actions per day (account safety)
- Never send identical messages (always personalize the signal reference)
- CEO account only — not from sales reps
- LinkedIn is for starting conversations, not closing deals

## Email Sequence Structure (3-Touch Recommended)

### Email 1: Signal + Pattern + CTA
Full signal-referenced email as described above. Sent on Tuesday or Wednesday, 9-11am recipient time.

### Email 2: Value-Add Follow-Up (5-7 days later)
Share something useful — a relevant data architecture pattern, a blog post about their specific challenge, or a framework. Short: 3-4 sentences plus the link/resource. No hard CTA, just "thought this might be relevant."

### Email 3: Soft Close (5-7 days after Email 2)
Brief, direct: "I shared a couple of thoughts about [Company]'s data buildout. If the timing isn't right, no worries — happy to reconnect when it makes sense. But if there's a specific challenge I can help think through, I'm here."

### Sequence Rules
- Stop if they reply (even negatively — acknowledge and respect)
- No more than 3 emails in a cold sequence
- Minimum 5 days between touches
- Personalize every email — no mass-blast identical sequences
