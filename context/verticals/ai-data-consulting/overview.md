# AI & Data Consulting — Vertical Overview

## What This Vertical Covers

AI & Data Consulting is CirrusLabs' advisory and implementation practice for companies building or scaling their AI, machine learning, and data platform capabilities. Unlike staffing (placing individual contributors) or cloud software delivery (building and shipping product), this vertical delivers **strategic outcomes**: data strategies, ML roadmaps, platform architectures, and hands-on implementation led by senior practitioners.

Core engagement types:
- **AI/ML Strategy & Roadmap** — Assess readiness, define use cases, prioritize by ROI, build execution plan
- **Data Platform Modernization** — Migrate from legacy ETL to modern cloud-native data stacks (Snowflake, Databricks, dbt, Airflow)
- **ML Engineering & MLOps** — Take models from notebooks to production with monitoring, retraining, and governance
- **Fractional Data Leadership** — Interim CDO/VP Data for companies that need senior direction before (or instead of) a full-time hire
- **Analytics Strategy** — Define KPI frameworks, build self-serve analytics, reduce dashboard sprawl
- **Data Quality & Governance** — Establish data contracts, lineage, cataloging, and trust frameworks

## How This Differs from Staffing

| Dimension | Staffing | AI & Data Consulting |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| Deliverable | People (hours/month) | Outcomes (strategy, architecture, working systems) |
| Engagement model | Staff augmentation | Project-based or fractional leadership |
| Buyer mindset | "I need 3 data engineers" | "I need my data platform rebuilt" |
| Pricing | Hourly/monthly rate | Project milestone or retainer |
| Success metric | Seat filled, retention | Business outcome delivered |
| CirrusLabs role | Talent supplier | Trusted advisor + implementer |

## How This Differs from Cloud Software Delivery

Cloud delivery builds customer-facing software products. AI & Data Consulting builds the **internal data infrastructure, models, and analytics capabilities** that power those products. The buyer is typically a data/analytics leader, not a product or engineering VP.

## Market Positioning

CirrusLabs occupies the space between:
- **Big 4 / McKinsey** — Expensive, strategy-heavy, light on implementation
- **Pure staffing firms** — Bodies without strategy or architecture ownership
- **Niche ML boutiques** — Deep on models, weak on data platform and organizational change

Our positioning: **Practitioner-led AI & data consulting that bridges strategy and implementation.** We don't hand you a deck and leave. We build alongside your team and transfer knowledge so you're self-sufficient.

## Key Metrics for This Vertical

- **Average engagement size:** $75K-$300K (3-6 month projects)
- **Typical team:** 2-4 senior practitioners (architect + engineers + PM)
- **Win rate indicator:** Engagements where we identify a quick win in the first 2 weeks convert to extensions at high rates
- **Expansion pattern:** Strategy engagement leads to implementation project leads to fractional leadership retainer
- **Target pipeline:** 8-12 qualified conversations per quarter

## Ideal Signal Profile

The strongest leads for this vertical show a combination of:
1. Recently funded (Series B+ or growth equity) with AI/data ambitions stated publicly
2. Hiring for their first senior data role (CDO, VP Data, Head of ML)
3. Adopted modern data tools (Snowflake, Databricks) but struggling to get value from them
4. Public statements from leadership about AI transformation
5. Small or nonexistent data team relative to company size

## Revenue Model

- **Discovery/assessment:** $15K-$30K (2-4 weeks, leads to larger engagement)
- **Strategy & roadmap:** $40K-$80K (4-8 weeks)
- **Implementation project:** $100K-$300K (3-6 months)
- **Fractional leadership:** $25K-$40K/month retainer
- **Ongoing advisory:** $8K-$15K/month

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor Type | Strength | Weakness We Exploit |
|----------------|----------|-------------------|
| Big 4 (Deloitte, Accenture) | Brand, scale | Expensive, junior-heavy teams, slow |
| Boutique ML firms | Deep technical | Narrow scope, won't touch data platform |
| Staffing firms | Speed, flexibility | No strategy, no architecture ownership |
| Cloud vendors (AWS/GCP PS) | Platform depth | Vendor lock-in bias, not outcome-oriented |
| Internal build | Cultural fit | Slow to hire, expensive mistakes, no benchmarks |

## Key Principle

Every AI & data consulting engagement must deliver a **tangible artifact or working system** within the first 30 days. Strategy decks alone are not enough. The fastest path to trust — and to expansion — is demonstrating hands-on competence early.
