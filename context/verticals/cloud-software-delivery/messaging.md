# Cloud & Software Delivery — Messaging Framework

## Core Positioning Statement

CirrusLabs delivers cloud engineering teams that ship production code in your stack, on your timeline — not headcount that needs managing, and not consultants who deliver slide decks.

## Tone and Voice

**Engineering-credible, outcome-focused, direct.**

- Write like an engineering leader, not a salesperson
- Reference specific technologies, not vague "digital transformation" language
- Lead with the problem you solve, not your company description
- Be specific about what you deliver and how fast
- Avoid: "synergy," "leverage," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "innovative solutions"
- Use: deployment frequency, release cycle, migration timeline, infrastructure automation, production uptime

## Differentiation Messaging

### vs. Staffing Agencies
**Key message:** "We deliver outcomes, not headcount."

- Staffing puts a body in a seat. We own delivery milestones.
- Staffing bills hours. We commit to shipped software.
- Staffing says "here's a DevOps engineer." We say "your Kubernetes migration will be complete in 12 weeks."
- When a staffing contractor leaves, you lose their knowledge. When our engagement completes, you keep the infrastructure, documentation, and trained team.

### vs. Consulting Firms
**Key message:** "We build and ship, not advise and leave."

- Consulting delivers a strategy deck and a recommendations document. We deliver running infrastructure.
- Consulting says "you should adopt Kubernetes." We migrate your first 10 services and train your team to handle the rest.
- Consulting engagement ends with a PowerPoint. Ours ends with production deployments.
- We don't bill for discovery workshops and architecture reviews as standalone deliverables — they're built into delivery.

### vs. Offshore Development Shops
**Key message:** "Engineering partnership, not outsourced labor."

- Offshore shops work from specs thrown over a wall. We embed in your team, your standups, your repos.
- Offshore delivery means timezone gaps and communication overhead. Our teams overlap your working hours and join your Slack channels.
- Offshore means reviewing every line of code. Our engineers make architecture decisions and mentor your team.
- We follow your engineering standards, not our own templates.

## Value Propositions by Persona

### For VP Engineering
**Primary message:** Ship your roadmap faster without the 6-month hiring cycle.

- "Your product roadmap is 3 quarters behind because every DevOps role takes 4 months to fill. Our team starts shipping in 2 weeks."
- "You're choosing between product features and infrastructure work because the same team does both. We take infrastructure off your plate so your team ships features."
- "Every quarter you delay cloud migration, technical debt compounds. Our delivery team executes the migration while your team maintains momentum on product."

### For CTO
**Primary message:** Accelerate your cloud architecture with engineers who've done it before.

- "Your team is making cloud architecture decisions for the first time. Ours has migrated hundreds of services across AWS, Azure, and GCP."
- "You need Kubernetes expertise for 6 months, not forever. Our team builds the platform, documents it, and transfers knowledge so yours can operate it."
- "Cloud cost overruns happen when infrastructure is built without production patterns. We bring battle-tested architecture that scales without surprise bills."

### For Director of Platform Engineering
**Primary message:** Unblock your platform roadmap with engineers who work in your tools.

- "Your team has a 12-month platform roadmap and 3 engineers. Our team adds capacity without the recruiting, onboarding, and ramp-up time."
- "We work in your Terraform repos, your CI/CD pipelines, your monitoring stack. No separate environments, no handoff documents."
- "Your observability stack has gaps that cause 2am incident calls. We build the dashboards, alerts, and runbooks that let your team sleep."

### For Head of DevOps / SRE
**Primary message:** Get the hands-on infrastructure help your team has been asking for.

- "You've had 'implement service mesh' on the roadmap for 6 months. We'll have it running in production in 4 weeks."
- "Your Terraform modules need a refactor but there's always a higher priority. Our team handles the modernization while yours keeps the lights on."
- "We write the same quality of infrastructure code your team does — reviewed against your standards, merged through your process."

## Subject Line Patterns

Subject lines for Cloud & Software Delivery should reference the specific signal or technology, not generic cloud buzzwords.

**Signal-referencing patterns:**
- "{Company}'s Kubernetes migration — faster path"
- "Saw {Company} is hiring 4 DevOps engineers"
- "Re: {Company}'s cloud platform buildout"
- "{Company} + cloud-native delivery"
- "Question about {Company}'s infrastructure roadmap"

**Outcome-referencing patterns:**
- "Monthly to daily deploys in 8 weeks"
- "Your DevOps backlog, done in Q2"
- "Ship the platform, keep the team"
- "{Company}'s deployment pipeline — quick win"

**Avoid these patterns:**
- "Cloud transformation solutions for {Company}" (too generic)
- "Partnership opportunity" (means nothing)
- "CirrusLabs introduction" (nobody cares about your company name)
- "Innovative cloud services" (buzzword soup)

## Opening Hook Patterns

The first sentence must reference something specific to the prospect's situation.

**Signal-based hooks:**
- "I noticed {Company} has [N] open DevOps roles — scaling infrastructure teams is brutal right now."
- "Saw {Company} is adopting Kubernetes — the migration from [current orchestration] is one of the harder infrastructure transitions."
- "{Company}'s job posts mention Terraform and cloud migration — sounds like you're in the middle of a platform modernization."

**Problem-based hooks:**
- "Most mid-market engineering teams I talk to are stuck choosing between product features and infrastructure work."
- "The typical cloud migration takes 2x longer than planned because internal teams are still maintaining the old system while building the new one."
- "Companies your size usually have 3 DevOps engineers trying to do the work of 8."

**Do NOT open with:**
- "I'm [Name] from CirrusLabs and we help companies with cloud transformation." (nobody reads past this)
- "I hope this email finds you well." (instant delete)
- "I wanted to reach out because..." (wastes the first sentence)

## Proof Insertion Points

Every email should include one proof point. Insert after the problem statement, before the CTA.

**Placement pattern:**
1. Signal-based opening (1-2 sentences)
2. Problem articulation (1-2 sentences)
3. **Proof point** (1 sentence)
4. CTA (1 sentence)

**Proof point types for this vertical:**
- Delivery speed: "We typically have engineers shipping PRs in your codebase within the first two weeks."
- Outcome metric: "Our last platform engineering engagement increased deployment frequency from monthly to daily within the first quarter."
- Methodology: "We embed directly in your team's workflows — same repos, same standups, same CI/CD pipeline."
- Risk reduction: "We start with a 4-week accelerator so you can evaluate delivery quality before committing to a longer engagement."

## CTA Patterns

CTAs should be low-commitment and specific.

**Strong CTAs:**
- "Would it be useful to see how we've handled [specific challenge] for a similar company? Happy to share the approach in a 20-minute call."
- "If {Company} is considering external delivery support for the Kubernetes migration, I can walk through our typical engagement structure."
- "Would a 4-week platform accelerator be worth exploring? We scope these as a low-risk way to evaluate fit."

**Avoid:**
- "Let me know if you'd like to learn more about our services." (too vague)
- "Can I get 30 minutes on your calendar?" (too aggressive, no value offered)
- "Would you be open to a quick chat?" (what about?)

## Email Length Guidelines

- **Total email length:** 80-120 words maximum
- **Subject line:** 5-8 words
- **Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences each, maximum 3 paragraphs
- **One proof point per email** — don't stack multiple claims
- **One CTA per email** — don't give multiple options

## LinkedIn Message Adaptation

LinkedIn DMs must be even shorter (40-60 words) and more conversational.

- Drop the subject line
- Use first name only
- Reference their specific role or a post they shared
- Single sentence proof point
- Question-based CTA ("curious if you've looked at external delivery teams for the platform work?")
- **Never copy-paste email content into LinkedIn** — it reads as spam
