# Staffing Vertical — Messaging Framework

## Core Positioning Statement

CirrusLabs places pre-vetted data engineers, ML engineers, and cloud architects on contract and contract-to-hire engagements — typically delivering first qualified candidates within one week. We are a specialized technical staffing firm, not a generalist recruiting agency.

---

## Value Propositions by Persona

### VP of Engineering / Head of Engineering
**Primary message:** "Fill your specialized engineering roles in weeks, not months, with candidates who pass technical screens before you ever see them."
- Speed: Pre-vetted bench means first candidates in 5-7 business days
- Quality: Technical assessments calibrated to the role, not generic coding tests
- Flexibility: Scale up for project ramps, convert to FTE when the fit is proven
- Low overhead: We handle sourcing, screening, and scheduling — you just interview the finalists

### CTO / Co-founder
**Primary message:** "Accelerate your engineering roadmap without the 3-month lag of internal recruiting for specialized roles."
- Strategic framing: Staffing as a tool to de-risk timelines, not just fill seats
- Contract-to-hire: Evaluate engineers in production before committing to FTE offers
- Domain expertise: Deep bench in data/ML/cloud — not generalist engineers
- Cost comparison: A 3-month vacancy costs more than a staffing markup

### Engineering Manager
**Primary message:** "Get engineers who can ship code in your stack from day one — not resumes that look good but cannot pass your technical bar."
- Stack specificity: Candidates matched to your exact technology requirements
- Ramp speed: Engineers who have worked in similar environments and can be productive fast
- Low interview burden: We pre-screen rigorously so you interview fewer, better candidates
- Team fit: We assess for collaboration style and communication, not just technical skill

### Talent Acquisition / Recruiting Lead
**Primary message:** "Supplement your internal recruiting for the hardest-to-fill engineering roles — we handle the niche specialties, you focus on volume."
- Complementary positioning: We augment your team, not replace it
- Specific role coverage: Data engineers, ML engineers, cloud architects — the roles that take longest to fill
- Process alignment: We work within your ATS, interview process, and timelines
- Metrics-friendly: Faster time-to-fill and better submittal-to-interview ratios for specialized roles

## Subject Line Patterns

### Signal-Specific (Highest Open Rate)
- "[Company] + [Role] — candidates ready this week"
- "Saw you're hiring [specific role]s"
- "Re: [Company]'s [role] search"
- "[Number] [role] openings — we can help"

### Urgency-Based
- "[Role] has been open [X] weeks — shortcut?"
- "Filling [role] before Q[X]"
- "Skip the 3-month recruiting cycle for [role]"

### Value-First
- "3 pre-vetted [role] profiles for [Company]"
- "[Role] candidates who've done this before"
- "How [similar companies] fill [role] in 2 weeks"

### Avoid These Subject Lines
- "Partnering with [Company]" — too vague, reads as spam
- "Top talent for your team" — generic, zero specificity
- "Staffing solutions for [Company]" — corporate filler
- "Let's connect!" — gives no reason to open
- Any subject line that does not reference the company, role, or a specific signal

## Opening Hook Patterns

### The Signal Reference (Best)
> I noticed [Company] has [number] open [role] positions — and the [specific role] has been posted since [date]. That's a tough hire through internal channels alone.

### The Speed Promise
> We have pre-vetted [role] candidates who can interview with your team this week. No 6-week sourcing cycle — these are engineers already on our bench.

### The Specificity Play
> Your job post for a [role] mentions [specific technology]. We have [number] engineers with production experience in [that technology] who are available for contract work starting [timeframe].

### The Peer Reference
> We've been placing [role]s at [similar company type — e.g., "Series B SaaS companies"] for the past year. The pattern is always the same: internal recruiting handles volume roles, but specialized [role]s take 2-3x longer.

### Avoid These Openings
- "I hope this email finds you well" — immediate delete
- "I wanted to introduce myself" — self-centered, not value-centered
- "We are a leading staffing firm" — nobody cares about your positioning in the opening line
- "I'd love to learn about your hiring needs" — gives nothing, asks for everything

## Proof Insertion Points

Every email should contain at least one proof element. Insert proof at these natural points:

### After the hook (credibility builder)
> ...We typically fill similar roles in 2-3 weeks. [Proof point about speed or fill rate]

### Before the CTA (risk reducer)
> [Proof point about candidate quality or retention]. Want to see a few profiles?

### As a parenthetical (casual credibility)
> We specialize in data engineering placements (our candidates have a 90%+ first-interview pass rate with similar companies).

## CTA Patterns

### Low-friction (Best for First Touch)
- "Want me to send over 2-3 anonymized profiles to review?"
- "Worth a 10-minute call to see if we have candidates that fit?"
- "Should I share a couple of profiles that match your [role] req?"

### Medium-friction
- "Can I set up a quick call this week to understand the role better?"
- "Would it help to see how we've filled similar roles for [peer companies]?"

### High-friction (Only for Warm Leads)
- "Want to schedule interviews with [number] candidates next week?"
- "Ready to kick off? We can have candidates in front of your team by [date]."

### CTA Rules
- Always offer something concrete (profiles, a call with a time bound, a specific next step)
- Never ask "Can I tell you more about our services?" — that is a meeting about you, not about them
- First-touch CTAs should require minimal commitment (review profiles, 10-minute call)
- Include specific availability: "Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon work?" beats "let me know when works"

## Tone Guidance

### Do
- Write like a technical peer, not a salesperson
- Be direct and concise (100-150 words for email body)
- Reference specific technologies, not just role titles
- Acknowledge that they get a lot of recruiter spam (and differentiate from it)
- Use concrete numbers where possible (time-to-fill, candidate count, availability)

### Do Not
- Use corporate buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "best-in-class talent")
- Write paragraphs longer than 3 sentences
- Use exclamation points
- Promise things you cannot deliver ("guaranteed hire in 48 hours")
- Be overly casual or use slang ("hey!", "just circling back")
- Include your company's full history or capabilities overview — nobody reads it

## Differentiation Angles vs. Competitors

### vs. Generalist Staffing Agencies (Robert Half, Insight Global, TEKsystems)
- **Their weakness:** Volume-first model, keyword matching, low technical vetting
- **Our angle:** "We only place data, ML, and cloud engineers. Every candidate passes architecture and coding assessments before we submit them."

### vs. Internal Recruiting
- **Their limitation:** Effective for common roles, slow for specialized/niche engineering
- **Our angle:** "Internal recruiting handles volume roles well. We complement that for the specialized roles that take 3x longer to fill."

### vs. Offshore Staffing
- **Their tradeoff:** Lower cost but timezone friction, communication overhead, and often lower seniority
- **Our angle:** "Our engineers work your timezone, attend your standups, and merge PRs in your codebase. No handoff layer, no communication overhead."

### vs. Consulting Firms Doing Staff Aug
- **Their model:** Higher day rates, project-oriented framing, longer sales cycles
- **Our angle:** "You don't need a consulting engagement. You need two more data engineers for 6 months. We make that simple."

## LinkedIn DM Guidelines

LinkedIn messages are secondary to email for staffing outreach. Use only for:
- Following up on an unanswered email (once, after 5-7 days)
- Reaching people whose email is not available
- Responding to inbound connection requests

### DM Rules
- Maximum 3-4 sentences
- Reference the same signal as the email
- Never copy-paste the email — write a shorter, more conversational version
- Do not pitch in the connection request message — connect first, message after
- Maximum 5-10 LinkedIn outreach actions per day (CEO account safety)
