# Staffing Vertical Overview

## What Staffing Means for CirrusLabs

CirrusLabs staffing places technical specialists — data engineers, ML engineers, cloud architects, software developers — on contract and contract-to-hire engagements. We are not a consulting firm delivering projects. We are not a managed services provider running operations. We supply vetted, production-ready engineers who embed into client teams and ship code from week one.

The distinction matters for positioning: staffing clients buy **people**, not **projects**. They have the work defined, the team structure in place, and the technical leadership to manage. What they lack is qualified talent available on their timeline.

## Market Positioning

CirrusLabs operates in the technical staffing segment focused on:

- **Specialization over breadth:** Data/ML, cloud infrastructure, and backend engineering — not general IT staffing
- **Speed over volume:** Filling 1-5 specialized roles in weeks, not flooding inboxes with 50 generic resumes
- **Technical vetting over resume forwarding:** Candidates are assessed on architecture, code quality, and system design before submission
- **Flexible engagement models:** Contract (3-12 months), contract-to-hire, and team augmentation

We compete against internal recruiting teams (slow for niche roles), generalist staffing agencies (volume-first, low signal-to-noise), and offshore staffing shops (timezone and quality tradeoffs).

## How Staffing Differs from Consulting and Delivery

| Dimension | Staffing | Consulting | Delivery |
|-----------|----------|------------|----------|
| What the client buys | Individual engineers | Expert advice + strategy | Completed project/product |
| Who manages the work | Client's engineering leadership | Consulting firm | Delivery firm |
| Success metric | Time-to-fill, retention, ramp speed | Recommendations adopted | Project shipped on time |
| Pricing model | Hourly/weekly bill rate | Day rate or project fee | Fixed price or T&M |
| Relationship duration | 3-12 months per placement | 2-8 week engagements | Project lifecycle |
| Client profile | Has team, needs more hands | Needs direction or expertise | Needs execution capacity |

This distinction drives everything downstream: ICP selection, messaging, objection handling, and proof points.

## Core Value Proposition

CirrusLabs fills specialized engineering roles faster than internal recruiting and with higher technical quality than generalist staffing agencies.

Three pillars:

1. **Speed:** Pre-vetted bench of data/ML/cloud engineers means first qualified candidates submitted within 5-7 business days, not 6-8 weeks
2. **Technical depth:** Every candidate passes architecture and coding assessments calibrated to the role — clients interview engineers, not resumes
3. **Flexibility:** Contract, contract-to-hire, and team augmentation models — scale up for project ramps, convert when the fit is proven

## Key Metrics That Matter

For the staffing vertical, track these at the campaign and portfolio level:

### Operational Metrics
- **Time-to-fill:** Days from req open to candidate start (target: < 21 business days)
- **Submittal-to-interview ratio:** What percentage of submitted candidates get interviews (target: > 60%)
- **Interview-to-offer ratio:** Conversion from interview to offer (target: > 30%)
- **Offer acceptance rate:** Candidates who accept (target: > 85%)
- **First-week retention:** Candidate still engaged after first week (target: > 95%)

### Campaign Metrics
- **Email reply rate:** Target > 8% for staffing outreach
- **Meeting conversion:** Target > 2% of contacted leads
- **Req-to-engagement:** Percentage of initial conversations that lead to a staffing req
- **Average bill rate:** Revenue per hour per placement
- **Gross margin per placement:** Bill rate minus pay rate

### Flywheel Metrics
- **Repeat client rate:** Percentage of clients who place a second req within 12 months
- **Referral rate:** New clients sourced from existing client introductions
- **Candidate redeployment:** Contractors who move from one client engagement to another

## Vertical-Specific Considerations

- **Seasonality:** Q1 and Q3 are peak hiring windows for tech companies; Q4 slows as budgets finalize
- **Competing priorities:** Engineering leaders are busy — outreach must be concise and signal-specific
- **Trust threshold:** Staffing has a reputation problem; proof points and specificity overcome "just another recruiter" skepticism
- **Candidate quality is the product:** Every interaction with a client reinforces or erodes the brand based on who we put in front of them
